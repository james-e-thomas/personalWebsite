In my line of work - and economics more generally - regression models are common. But without training in economics or statistics, understanding the regression outputs of say, a competition authority, can be complicated. This short note tries to break things down as simply as possible:

  1. what a regression is; and
  2. how regressions should be interpreted.

Alternatively, there is another valid approach which is ignoring this post, overlooking anything that looks like it contains confusing numbers and hoping that the authority has done a good job with the explanatory text!

What is a regression?

A regression model is a statistical model that estimates the relationship between an ‘outcome’ variable (e.g. price) and ‘predictor’ variable(s) (e.g. cost or the presence of a cartel). It seeks to assess the impact of a change in cost or the presence of a cartel on the price of a good.
